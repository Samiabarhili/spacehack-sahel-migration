# SpaceHack 2025 – Environmental Migration in the Sahel Region

---

## Project Context

The **Sahel region** in Africa is a semi-arid area that is highly vulnerable to **climate change**, **desertification**, and **resource scarcity**.

These environmental degradations contribute to **population displacement** and **social tensions**, making the region an important case study for understanding the links between environmental change and human migration.

---

## Objective

The goal of this project was to use **satellite data** to:

- Monitor environmental changes (vegetation, water resources, drought, precipitation)
- Explore potential relationships between **environmental stress** and **population movements**
- Identify possible directions for **local climate resilience strategies**

---

## Data and Tools

**Main platform:**  
Google Earth Engine (GEE)

**Datasets used:**

- NDVI vegetation index (MODIS)
- Precipitation data (GPM, CHIRPS)
- Surface water dynamics (JRC)
- Soil moisture (ERA5)
- Land surface temperature (MODIS)
- Population density (GPWv4)
- Urbanization data (GHSL)

> Data processing and analysis were performed directly within **Google Earth Engine using scripts that were not preserved**.  
> See the `sources.md` file for a detailed list of datasets used.

---

## Key Findings

- **Vegetation loss** observed in several areas of the Sahel
- **Decrease in precipitation trends** between 2000 and 2020
- **Unexpected increase in surface water**, potentially linked to physical processes such as erosion or hydrological changes
- Partial correlations identified between **drought conditions, vegetation loss, and migration patterns**

---

## Limitations

- Environmental data alone cannot explain migration dynamics without considering **historical, political, and social contexts**.
- The **GEE scripts were not archived**, only the methodological approach is documented.
- Observed correlations between environment and migration should therefore be interpreted **with caution**.

---

## Recommendations

- Combine satellite observations with **human datasets** (census data, conflict databases, field surveys)
- Develop **environmental and human risk maps** for the Sahel region
- Integrate satellite-based monitoring into **climate adaptation and public policy strategies**
